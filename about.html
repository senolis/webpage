About me

I'm a Solution Architect and Engineering Leader with over a decade of experience working on systems where the stakes are real — insurance platforms, industrial IoT, large-scale enterprise integrations. The kind of environments where a poor architecture decision doesn't just slow you down; it costs you years.

My background sits at the intersection of technical depth and delivery. I've led architecture work across organisations, shaped technical strategy at the leadership level, and worked closely with engineering teams to translate direction into something they can actually build. I understand both sides of that conversation — and I've spent a long time getting better at bridging them.

Over the years I've developed a strong focus on quality and observability — not as afterthoughts, but as foundational concerns. I believe that how a system behaves in production is at least as important as how it looks on a whiteboard. I've seen too many projects succeed on paper and fail in practice to think otherwise.

I work independently because it lets me focus on the problem in front of me without the overhead of a large organisation. It also means the person you speak with is the person doing the work. There's no handoff to a more junior team after the proposal is signed.

I take on both focused engagements — a specific decision to work through, an architecture to review, a strategy to pressure-test — and longer embedded roles where a team needs sustained technical leadership. What I'm drawn to, in either case, is work that has genuine impact: problems that matter to the people working on them, and outcomes that hold up over time.

If any of that sounds like what you're looking for, I'm happy to have a conversation. No commitment, no pitch — just a direct discussion about whether there's a fit.
